# CTAs — Standard

> **How to use:** Standard CTAs close the video with a clear action request.
> They should feel like a natural continuation of the conversation, not an ad.

---

## Standard CTA List

1. "If this made sense, follow me and I'll keep going."
2. "Save this so you don't go back to the mistake tomorrow."
3. "Would you do this today? Comment 'yes' or 'no.'"
4. "Tell me your niche and I'll adapt this example."
5. "Want the checklist version? Comment 'CHECK.'"
6. "Want part 2? I'll make it, but you have to ask."
7. "Send this to someone who needs to stop making this mistake."
8. "If you want the full step-by-step, comment 'WANT.'"
9. "If you've been through this, comment 'me too.'"
10. "If you disagree, perfect: comment and explain."
11. "Want me to review your hook? Drop it here."
12. "Want me to create 5 VPBs for your idea? Send me the topic."
13. "Save and test for 3 days."
14. "If you want to grow without improvising, stick around."
15. "If you want a real example for your niche, comment [NICHE]."
16. "Want me to turn this into a series? I'll build it."
17. "If this helped, share it with a stubborn friend."
18. "Comment 'SCRIPT' and I'll give you a template."
19. "If you want to stop freezing up, you need this."
20. "If you have a question, tell me what it is."

---

## CTA Categories

### Simple Engagement
- Asks for like, comment, or save
- "Save this so you don't go back to the mistake tomorrow."

### Continuity
- Promises more content in exchange for engagement
- "Want part 2? I'll make it, but you have to ask."

### Personalization
- Offers to adapt content to specific case
- "Tell me your niche and I'll adapt this example."

### Sharing
- Asks to send to someone
- "Send this to someone who needs to stop making this mistake."

### Dialogue
- Opens conversation in comments
- "If you disagree, perfect: comment and explain."

---

## Usage Tips

- Standard CTA works best after content that delivered real value
- Be specific in what you ask (not "comment below," but "comment 'CHECK'")
- Avoid multiple CTAs—choose one focus per video
