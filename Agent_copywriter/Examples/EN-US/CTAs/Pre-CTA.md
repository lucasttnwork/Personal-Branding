# CTAs — Pre-CTA (Mid-Video)

> **How to use:** Pre-CTAs appear mid-video to engage before the end.
> They work as "checkpoints" and increase retention.

---

## Pre-CTA List

1. "If this already made sense, save it now."
2. "Comment [KEYWORD] and I'll send you the link/template."
3. "If you're on team procrastinator, comment 'me.'"
4. "If you want the full list, comment 'LIST.'"
5. "If you want me to adapt this to your case, comment your niche."
6. "If you're watching this far, it's because this hit."
7. "If you want the practical part, stay with me."
8. "If you want my framework, comment 'FRAME.'"
9. "If you want the file, comment 'PDF.'"
10. "If you've made this mistake, comment 'been there.'"
11. "If you want me to hold you accountable next time, comment 'check on me.'"
12. "If you want an example with numbers, comment 'numbers.'"
13. "If you want me to build your series, comment 'series.'"
14. "If this helped, save it—seriously."
15. "If you got the point, drop a ✅."

---

## When to Use Pre-CTA

### After Revealing the Problem
- "If you're watching this far, it's because this hit."
- Marks identification and increases commitment to watch till the end

### Before the Solution
- "If you want the practical part, stay with me."
- Creates anticipation and keeps the viewer

### After First Tip
- "If this already made sense, save it now."
- Captures engagement before the viewer leaves

### At the Turning Point
- "If you've made this mistake, comment 'been there.'"
- Generates identification and comments

---

## Positioning Strategy

```
[0:00-0:05]  → Hook
[0:05-0:15]  → Development
[0:15-0:20]  → PRE-CTA ← here
[0:20-0:40]  → Solution/Main content
[0:40-0:50]  → Final CTA
```

---

## Usage Tips

- Pre-CTA doesn't replace final CTA—they're complementary
- Use at the moment of highest tension/identification
- Keep it brief—pre-CTA can't break the rhythm
- Combine with on-screen text to reinforce the keyword
