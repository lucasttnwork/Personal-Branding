# Transitions — To CTA

> **How to use:** CTA transitions prepare the viewer for the final action.
> They're the bridge between content and the engagement ask.

---

## CTA Transition List

1. "If this hit home, do one thing for me…"
2. "If this made sense, you already know what to do."
3. "Want me to adapt this to your case?"
4. "Comment [word] and I'll send you the path."
5. "Save this so you don't go back to the mistake tomorrow."
6. "If you want part 2, give me a signal here."
7. "Want me to turn this into a checklist?"
8. "Tell me your niche and I'll make a specific example."
9. "If you want my full framework…"
10. "If you're ready to stop improvising…"
11. "If you want to see this working in practice…"
12. "If you want the ready-made script…"
13. "If you want the VPBs from this video…"
14. "If you want the template I use…"
15. "If you want me to rewrite your hook…"
16. "Comment 'WANT' and I'll continue."
17. "If you don't want it, ignore. But don't complain later."
18. "If you have a question, great…"
19. "I'll close with a question:"
20. "Now I'll loop back to the beginning…"

---

## Types of CTA Transitions

### Conditional (If you...)
- Qualifies the viewer before asking for action
- "If this made sense, you already know what to do."

### Invitation (Want me to...)
- Offers additional value in exchange for engagement
- "Want me to turn this into a checklist?"

### Challenge (Tell me / Comment)
- Asks for active participation
- "Tell me your niche and I'll make a specific example."

### Looping (Now I'll loop...)
- Connects the ending to the beginning for rewatch
- "Now I'll loop back to the beginning…"

---

## Usage Tips

- CTA transition should feel like a natural continuation, not a break
- Use conversational tone—avoid "salesperson" vibes
- Combine with pause/direct eye contact before the final CTA
