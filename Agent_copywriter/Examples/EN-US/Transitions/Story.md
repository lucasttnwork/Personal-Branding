# Transitions — Story

> **How to use:** Story transitions create narrative immersion.
> Used to build context, mark turning points, and create emotional connection.

---

## Story Transition List

1. "Sit down, here comes the context."
2. "I remember exactly where I was."
3. "I thought it was just a phase."
4. "Then a specific scene happened."
5. "That's when it clicked."
6. "I was stubborn for [time]."
7. "I tried [X] and failed spectacularly."
8. "I was on autopilot."
9. "I was fooling myself with 'productivity.'"
10. "I was waiting to 'feel like it.'"
11. "I was ashamed, not lazy."
12. "I realized I was using a sophisticated excuse."
13. "Then I changed one small thing."
14. "And the result was almost offensively simple."
15. "I became this person right here."
16. "I stopped fighting reality."
17. "I stopped romanticizing struggle."
18. "I stopped asking for permission."
19. "I stopped waiting for confidence."
20. "I started small. But every day."

---

## When to Use

- **To open context** → "Sit down, here comes the context."
- **To create immersion** → "I remember exactly where I was."
- **To mark turning point** → "That's when it clicked."
- **To show vulnerability** → "I was ashamed, not lazy."
- **To close the arc** → "And the result was almost offensively simple."

---

## Storytelling Structure

```
[Context] → I was on autopilot.
[Tension] → I tried [X] and failed spectacularly.
[Turning Point] → That's when it clicked.
[Change] → Then I changed one small thing.
[Result] → And the result was almost offensively simple.
```

---

## Usage Tips

- Use specific details (time, place, phrase) for authenticity
- Story transitions need slower pacing—take a breath
- Combine with "flashback" VPBs or scene changes
