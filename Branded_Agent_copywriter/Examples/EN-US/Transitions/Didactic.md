# Transitions — Didactic

> **How to use:** Didactic transitions organize information and guide reasoning.
> Used to explain, compare, or structure educational content.

---

## Didactic Transition List

1. "First: [step 1]. Then: [step 2]."
2. "The rule is: [rule]. Everything else is decoration."
3. "Think of it this way: [analogy]."
4. "Swap this for that: [A] → [B]."
5. "If you only remember one thing, remember this."
6. "Okay, let me draw this out."
7. "In plain English: …"
8. "This is the simplest test in the world."
9. "If you do it wrong, it goes bad like this: …"
10. "If you do it right, this happens: …"
11. "You want speed? Then cut this."
12. "You want quality? Then add this."
13. "Look at the sequence: …"
14. "Save that phrase."
15. "Now I'll give you a ridiculous example."
16. "Now I'll give you a real-world example."
17. "This works because…"
18. "The mistake is believing that…"
19. "The win is realizing that…"
20. "Now you choose: short-term pain or long-term pain."

---

## When to Use

- **To simplify** → "Think of it this way: [analogy]."
- **To structure** → "First: [step 1]. Then: [step 2]."
- **To compare** → "Swap this for that: [A] → [B]."
- **To exemplify** → "Now I'll give you a ridiculous example."
- **To reinforce** → "If you only remember one thing, remember this."

---

## Usage Tips

- Perfect for infotainment content and tutorials
- Combine with on-screen text to reinforce structure
- Use when content is dense and needs clarity
