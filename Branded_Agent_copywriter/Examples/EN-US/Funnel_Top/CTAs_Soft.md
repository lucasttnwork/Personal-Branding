# Soft CTAs - Top of Funnel (EN-US)

## Overview

Low-friction CTAs for the discovery phase. The goal is to generate initial engagement, increase reach, and create first touchpoints without asking for commitment. None of these CTAs ask for sales, appointments, or direct contact.

**Tone:** Casual, friendly, no pressure
**Objective:** Save, follow, comment, share
**Funnel stage:** Discovery (40%)

---

## CTAs by Category

### Category 1: Save (Retain for Later)

1. "Save this for later."
2. "Bookmark this for your next project."
3. "Save this. You'll need it."
4. "Keep this one. Seriously."
5. "Add this to your saved. Trust me."

### Category 2: Follow (Build Audience)

6. "Follow for part 2."
7. "Follow for more on premium digital presence."
8. "If this resonated, follow for more."
9. "Want more insights like this? Hit follow."
10. "Follow so you don't miss the next one."

### Category 3: Comment (Drive Engagement)

11. "Drop a comment if you've been through this."
12. "Comment 'ME' if this describes you."
13. "What's your industry? Drop it in the comments."
14. "Comment the biggest website mistake you've made."
15. "Disagree? Let me know in the comments."
16. "Drop your company name and I'll give my take."
17. "A or B? Comment your choice."
18. "Has this happened to you? Tell me about it."

### Category 4: Share (Increase Reach)

19. "Send this to a founder who needs to hear it."
20. "Tag a business owner who needs to see this."
21. "Share this with someone going through this."
22. "Send this to your co-founder. They need to see it."

### Category 5: Micro-Commitment (Simple Interaction)

23. "Drop a like if this hit home."
24. "Agree? Leave a like."
25. "If this described you, drop a fire emoji below."
26. "Double tap if you've lived this."

### Category 6: Continuity (Promise of More)

27. "Part 2 shows you how to fix it. Follow me."
28. "Tomorrow I'm showing the step-by-step. Turn on notifications."
29. "Next week I'm sharing a real case study. Don't miss it."
30. "This is just the beginning. Follow for the rest."

---

## When to Use

### By Format:
- **Reels:** CTAs 1-5 (save) + CTAs 6-10 (follow)
- **Carousels:** CTAs 1-5 (save) on last slide
- **Stories:** CTAs 23-26 (micro-commitment) with polls
- **Feed posts:** CTAs 11-18 (comment) for discussion
- **Lives:** CTAs 6-10 (follow) + CTAs 27-30 (continuity)

### By Objective:
- **Increase saves:** Use when content is educational and dense
- **Grow followers:** Use when content promises continuation
- **Boost comments:** Use when you want audience data
- **Drive shares:** Use when content is provocative or controversial

## Hook + CTA Combination Examples

### Combination 1: Educational
**Hook:** "90% of high-ticket business websites make this mistake."
**Content:** Explains the mistake (lack of conversion strategy)
**CTA:** "Save this to check if your site makes this mistake."

### Combination 2: Series
**Hook:** "The problem isn't your product. It's where you're showing it."
**Content:** Introduces Strategic Digital Dissonance concept
**CTA:** "Follow for part 2 where I show you how to diagnose this."

### Combination 3: Interactive
**Hook:** "If you're embarrassed to share your website with investors, this is for you."
**Content:** Discusses the importance of premium digital presence
**CTA:** "Drop 'GUILTY' in the comments if you've felt this. I read every one."

### Combination 4: Viral
**Hook:** "Your generic website is destroying your profit margins."
**Content:** Shows how amateur sites force price competition
**CTA:** "Tag a founder who needs to hear this before it's too late."

### Combination 5: Provocative
**Hook:** "You have a Ferrari with a lawnmower engine."
**Content:** Full analogy about design vs strategy
**CTA:** "Disagree? Let's debate in the comments. I'm open to being wrong."

## Usage Rules

1. **Never use more than one CTA per content piece** - choose the most relevant one
2. **Align CTA with content objective** - if you want saves, ask for saves
3. **Vary your CTAs** to avoid sounding repetitive
4. **Test different formulas** and measure which performs better
5. **Comment CTAs** generate audience data - use strategically
6. **Save CTAs** indicate valuable content - good signal for the algorithm
7. American audiences appreciate directness - avoid overly polite hedging
8. "Drop a" is more casual than "Leave a" - matches the discovery tone
