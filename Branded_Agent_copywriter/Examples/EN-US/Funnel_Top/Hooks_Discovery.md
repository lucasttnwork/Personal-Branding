# Discovery Hooks - Top of Funnel (EN-US)

## Overview

Hooks for the discovery phase, when the audience doesn't know Lucas Nogueira yet or isn't aware of the problem they face. The goal is to capture attention, spark curiosity, and validate the ICP's pain (James/Sarah - CEOs of premium B2B and B2C high-ticket businesses in USA, Canada, UK).

**Tone:** Casual, provocative, curiosity-driven
**Focus:** Problem (Strategic Digital Dissonance)
**Funnel proportion:** 40% of content

---

## Hooks by Category

### Category 1: Curiosity Gap

1. "Your website is driving away your best clients. And you don't even know it."
2. "90% of high-ticket business websites make this mistake. Yours probably does too."
3. "The reason your competitor closes more deals isn't price. It's this."
4. "You're losing $50,000 clients because of one detail nobody talks about."
5. "The problem isn't your product. It's where you're showing it."
6. "If your website doesn't do this in the first 3 seconds, you've already lost the sale."
7. "Why premium businesses never compete on price."
8. "What successful B2B companies know about websites that you don't."
9. "Your dashboard is costing you clients. Let me show you why."
10. "The invisible mistake that makes high-value businesses look amateur."

### Category 2: Identity Validation

11. "If you're embarrassed to share your website with investors, this is for you."
12. "This is for business owners who've spent money on agencies with nothing to show for it."
13. "You're too good to have a 'good enough' website. Agree?"
14. "Founders making serious revenue with a digital presence that doesn't match."
15. "If your service is premium but your site looks like a template, stop everything."
16. "For those tired of hearing 'I loved your pitch, but...'"
17. "Business owners who are authorities in their market but invisible online."
18. "If you charge premium prices and your website doesn't reflect that, we have a problem."
19. "You know your business is worth more than your website shows."
20. "For CEOs who are done losing deals because of how they look online."

### Category 3: Pain Amplification

21. "Your generic website is destroying your profit margins. I'll prove it."
22. "Every day with that website is money thrown away. Literally."
23. "While you read this, a competitor with a worse product is closing your client."
24. "You're paying for traffic to send people to a website that scares them off."
25. "The cost of an amateur website: lost clients, crushed margins, damaged reputation."
26. "Your website is the first impression. And it's saying you're 'just another option.'"
27. "9 out of 10 visitors leave your site thinking you're not for them."
28. "Your competitor with the inferior product closes more because they look more professional."
29. "That 'beautiful' website you paid for wasn't built to sell. It was built to look pretty."
30. "You have a Ferrari with a lawnmower engine. Stunning outside, no engineering inside."

---

## When to Use

- **Reels/TikTok:** Opening line (0-3 seconds)
- **Carousels:** Cover slide
- **Stories:** First frame with text
- **Feed posts:** First line of caption
- **YouTube Shorts:** Opening statement before logo

### Ideal Contexts:
- Content about common digital presence problems
- Comparisons between amateur vs professional websites
- Behind-the-scenes showing real website audits
- Provocative content about the digital marketing industry

## Hook + CTA Combination Examples

### Combination 1 (Curiosity + Soft CTA)
**Hook:** "Your website is driving away your best clients. And you don't even know it."
**CTA:** "Save this to check your own site later."

### Combination 2 (Identity + Soft CTA)
**Hook:** "If you're embarrassed to share your website with investors, this is for you."
**CTA:** "Follow for part 2 where I show you how to fix this."

### Combination 3 (Pain + Soft CTA)
**Hook:** "You have a Ferrari with a lawnmower engine. Stunning outside, no engineering inside."
**CTA:** "Drop a 'FERRARI' in the comments if you've been there."

### Combination 4 (Curiosity + Engagement)
**Hook:** "The reason your competitor closes more deals isn't price. It's this."
**CTA:** "What's your industry? Comment below and I'll tell you if you're making this mistake."

### Combination 5 (Identity + Save)
**Hook:** "This is for business owners who've spent money on agencies with nothing to show for it."
**CTA:** "Save this for when you're hiring your next project."

## Application Notes

1. **Curiosity Gap hooks** work best when the content actually delivers on the promise
2. **Identity Validation hooks** create emotional connection - use when content is more personal
3. **Pain Amplification hooks** are more aggressive - balance with solution-oriented content
4. All hooks should be adapted to format (written vs spoken)
5. Test variations by replacing technical terms with analogies when needed
6. American audiences respond well to direct, confident language - avoid hedging
7. Use specific dollar amounts when possible (more relatable than percentages alone)
