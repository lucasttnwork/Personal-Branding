# Terminology Glossary — Lucas Nogueira Brand (EN-US)

This document standardizes all terminology for the Lucas Nogueira brand. Use it as the absolute reference to ensure consistency across all communication.

---

## 1. Proprietary Brand Terms

These are UNIQUE terms created for the brand. **ALWAYS** use exactly as documented.

### 1.1 Problem (What we solve)

| Term EN-US | Term PT-BR | Definition | When to Use |
|------------|------------|------------|-------------|
| Strategic Digital Dissonance | Dissonancia Digital Estrategica | The failure to treat digital presence as a single, integrated system, resulting in disconnected pieces (design, technology, funnel) that cancel out sales objectives. | In ALL funnel stages when naming the main problem. Technical authority term. |
| Digital Perception Misalignment | Desalinhamento de Percepcao Digital | When high-value companies have a digital presence that looks amateur and untrustworthy, sabotaging sales and attracting the wrong clients. | More accessible variation for top of funnel. Describes the VISIBLE SYMPTOM of the problem. |

**Correct usage examples:**

> **Top of Funnel:** "What you have isn't a design problem. It's Digital Perception Misalignment — your site looks 3 levels below what you actually deliver."

> **Middle of Funnel:** "Strategic Digital Dissonance happens when designer, developer, and marketing don't follow the same playbook. The result? An orchestra where everyone plays a different tune."

> **Bottom of Funnel:** "Our system eliminates Strategic Digital Dissonance by integrating strategy, design, and technology into a single cohesive vision."

---

### 1.2 Solution (How we solve it)

| Term EN-US | Term PT-BR | Definition | When to Use |
|------------|------------|------------|-------------|
| Synchronous Conversion Architecture | Arquitetura de Conversao Sincronica | The methodology that unifies strategy, design, and technology into a single cohesive system, designed to solve Strategic Digital Dissonance. | Middle and bottom of funnel. This is the UNIQUE MECHANISM of the solution — the "engineering" behind the system. |

**Correct usage examples:**

> **Middle of Funnel (educational):** "Synchronous Conversion Architecture is, essentially, the strategic planning of every interaction a visitor will have with your site. We're not talking about design — we're talking about how every element works together to guide the person toward conversion."

## 2. Standardized Technical Terms

Industry terms that should be used consistently.

### 2.1 Metrics and KPIs

| Term | Definition for ICP | Usage Context |
|------|-------------------|---------------|
| Conversion rate | Percentage of visitors who take the desired action (fill form, book, purchase). | Educational and sales. Always explain with an example. |
| Bounce rate | Percentage of visitors who leave without interacting. | Educational. Well-known term in American market. |
| Qualified leads | Potential clients who have shown real interest and have purchasing capacity. | All stages. Differentiate from "cold leads." |
| SQLs (Sales Qualified Leads) | Leads ready for sales conversation. | B2B and technical materials. Explain on first mention. |
| MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue) | Monthly recurring revenue. | B2B SaaS. Well-known in target market. |
| ROI (Return on Investment) | Return on investment. | Bottom of funnel. Already known by ICP. |
| CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) | Cost to acquire a customer. | B2B and technical materials. Well-known term. |
| LTV (Lifetime Value) | Customer value over time. | B2B and technical materials. Well-known term. |

**Correct usage example:**

> "Your bounce rate is at 85%. In other words, out of every 100 people who land on your page, 85 leave without clicking anything. In practice, this means your traffic investment is literally going down the drain."

---

### 2.2 Digital System Elements

| Term | Definition for ICP | Usage Context |
|------|-------------------|---------------|
| Landing page | Page focused on a single conversion action. | All stages. Well-known term. |
| High-converting landing page | Landing page strategically optimized to maximize results. | Differentiate from "regular landing page." |
| Sales funnel / automation funnel | Strategic sequence that guides leads from first contact to sale. | Educational and sales. Funnel analogy is well-known. |
| Digital asset | Any digital property that generates value for the business (site, landing page, app). | Our preferred way of speaking. Avoid "website" alone. |
| Frontend | Visual and interactive part of an application. | Technical/SaaS contexts. |
| Dashboard | Control panel/data visualization. | Technical/SaaS contexts. |
| CRM | Customer relationship management system. | B2B. Explain if necessary. |
| Automation | Processes that run without manual intervention. | All stages. Well-known term. |

## 3. Official Brand Analogies

Use these analogies consistently. They are SIGNATURES of the communication.

| Analogy | Meaning | When to Use |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Ferrari with a Beetle engine** | Beautiful site on the outside, no conversion engineering inside. | Describe the problem memorably. Top and middle of funnel. |
| **Orchestra without a conductor** | Designer, developer, and marketing without strategic alignment — each "playing their own tune." | Explain Strategic Digital Dissonance. Middle of funnel. |
| **Luxury storefront with a warehouse interior** | Premium first impression, amateur experience. | Variation of the main analogy. |
| **Five-star restaurant with dive bar service** | Excellent design, terrible user experience. | Explain UX problems. |
| **Maze vs. well-lit corridor** | Confusing site vs. site with clear conversion architecture. | Explain conversion architecture. |

**Correct usage example:**

> "Your site is like a Ferrari with a Beetle engine. On the outside, it looks impressive. But inside? It's missing the conversion engineering that turns visitors into clients."

## 4. Prohibited Terms

Terms that should **NOT** be used and their alternatives.

| DON'T Use | Why | Use Instead |
|-----------|-----|-------------|
| "Corporate website" | Dated term, no differentiation | "Digital asset," "Digital headquarters," "Digital presence" |
| "Build a website" | Commoditizes the work | "Build an acquisition system," "Develop your digital asset" |
| "Web designer" | Diminishes scope of work | "Conversion architect," "Digital asset specialist" |
| "Freelancer" | Association with amateurism | "Consultant," "Specialist" |
| "Cheap/affordable" | Contradicts premium positioning | "Strategic investment," "Optimized value" |
| "The best" / "Amazing" | Empty superlatives | Prove with data or specificity |
| "Revolutionary" | Marketing cliche | Describe the concrete result |
| "Take your business to the next level" | Generic phrase with no meaning | Be specific about the result |
| "Complete solution" | Vague | Specify what's included |
| "Digital marketing agency" | Different from our positioning | "Strategic digital asset consultancy" |
| "Website creation" | Commoditizes | "Conversion system architecture" |

## 5. Usage Rules by Funnel Stage

### 5.1 Top of Funnel (Discovery)

**Recommended terms:**
- Digital Perception Misalignment (more accessible form)
- Analogies (Ferrari/Beetle, Orchestra without conductor)
- Bounce rate, Qualified leads

**Terms to avoid:**
- Technical jargon without explanation
- Full methodology names (too technical for discovery)
- Deliverable details

**Example:**
> "75% of premium company websites look like they were built in 2015. It's not a budget problem. It's Digital Perception Misalignment."

---

### 5.2 Middle of Funnel (Education)

**Recommended terms:**
- Strategic Digital Dissonance (full technical term)
- Synchronous Conversion Architecture
- F.O.C.O. Method with explanation of each step
- All analogies
- Technical terms WITH explanation

**Example:**
> "Strategic Digital Dissonance happens when you treat your site, landing pages, and automations as isolated components. The result? An orchestra where every musician plays a different tune."

---

### 5.3 Bottom of Funnel (Sales)

**Recommended terms:**
- Premium Acquisition System
- 24/7 Digital Sales Asset
- Strategic Conversion Blueprint
- Intelligent Automation Engine
- Premium Performance & Positioning Ironclad Guarantee
- ROI, Qualified leads, Conversion rate

**Example:**
> "The Premium Acquisition System includes: 24/7 Digital Sales Asset + Strategic Conversion Blueprint + Intelligent Automation Engine. Everything protected by our 90-day Ironclad Guarantee."

---

### 5.4 B2B Networking

**Recommended terms:**
- Technical terms peers understand (SQLs, MRR, CAC, LTV)
- Specialist-to-specialist analogies
- References to shared experiences

**Example:**
> "What I've found working with premium consultancies is that the problem is almost never CAC — it's qualification. Leads come in, but they're not ready. The site speaks 'to everyone,' so it attracts everyone."
